# Write a Pricing / Renewal Proposal like a Top Pricing-Strategy Partner

> Don't drop the price. Redefine what 'value' means before they do.

![pricing-renewal-proposal-deck methodology illustration](https://cdn1.genspark.ai/user-upload-image/slide_agent/v2-catalog-hero/043-simon-kucher-pricing.png)

## Why this skill works

- **The CFO's first slide is the customer's own realised value, in their own currency.** They came to argue list price; they're now arguing about whether to leave the value on the table. The frame is reset.
- **The discount conversation has fences.** Good-Better-Best with hard fences (volume, term, commitment) gives the seller something to trade *other than price*. Renewal discount creep stops because there's a structural alternative.
- **You bring last year's usage data and the CFO's pushback.** The realised-value baseline gets computed, the Willingness-to-Pay anchor gets built, and the output is three named pricing scenarios the CSM can defend line-by-line.

## Methodology cheat-sheet

**Value-Based Pricing × Monetizing Innovation × B2B Elements of Value** — three converging canons that together replace cost-plus and discount-anchored pricing with customer-value pricing.

1. **Nagle, Hogan, Zale — *The Strategy and Tactics of Pricing***[^1]. The discipline's standard reference. Value-based pricing starts from the customer's economic value of use, not the vendor's cost. The Reference Value + Differentiation Value formulation underpins slide 05 (Willingness-to-Pay anchor).

2. **Ramanujam & Tacke — *Monetizing Innovation***[^2] (top pricing-consultancy partners). Five-rule playbook: have the willingness-to-pay conversation *early*, design the product to price, segment by WTP not by demographics, design pricing as a separate product, and communicate value with discipline. The Good-Better-Best with real fences (slide 07) is the operational form of rule 3 (Segment).

3. **Bain — *The B2B Elements of Value***[^3]. Empirical hierarchy of 40 value elements across five tiers (Table Stakes, Functional, Ease of Doing Business, Individual, Inspirational). The Realised Value slide (03) is built by selecting the 3-5 highest-tier elements the customer has demonstrably received.

4. **Renewal-pricing operating practice** — Force Management[^4] and SaaStr empirical research[^5] on the structural difference between cost-plus renewal increases (which lose accounts) and value-anchored renewals (which expand them). The walk-away discipline (encoded in speaker notes) is from this body of practice.

## Before / After

### Opening a renewal conversation under price pressure

**Typical PPT template**

> **2025 Renewal Proposal**
>
> - Current ARR: $480,000
> - 2025 list price update: +22%
> - Proposed renewal ARR: $585,600
> - Multi-year discount available
> - Looking forward to another great year together!

**This skill's rewrite (slide 03, Realised Value)**

> **What you got in 2024 — measured in your numbers, not ours:**
>
> - **2,140 recruiter-hours returned to hiring managers** (Source: your HRIS time-tracking, exported Jan 6 2025)
> - **$1.8M of revenue enabled by zero-day onboarding** for 47 net-new hires (Source: your CRM bookings, attached as Appendix A)
> - **0 SOC 2 audit findings** vs the 4 findings on the legacy stack last year (Source: your auditor's Q4 letter)
>
> *Your CFO's question is "what is the price". Our question is "where are you putting this $4 of return".*

### Closing the renewal

**Typical PPT template**

> **Three Renewal Options**
>
> - Option A: 1-year, 22% increase
> - Option B: 1-year, 12% increase
> - Option C: 1-year, 5% increase (preferred partnership!)
> - We hope you'll choose Option C
> - Thank you

**This skill's rewrite (slide 07, Good-Better-Best with fences)**

> **Three real packages — none of them is "the same thing for less":**
>
> | Lever | Sustain | Expand | Compound |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | Term | 1 year | 2 years | 3 years |
> | Volume tier | current seats | +25% seats, prepaid | +50% seats, prepaid |
> | Premium SLA | standard | 99.9% + named CSM | 99.95% + executive sponsor |
> | New module: Compliance | not included | included Y2 | included Y1 |
> | Effective $/seat/mo | **$78** (list) | **$71** (-9%) | **$64** (-18%) |
>
> *Sustain matches what your CFO costed out. Expand and Compound are how you get the next $2M of return for the same effective cost.*

## What this skill produces

An 11-slide, 1920×1080 HTML deck for a 45-minute pricing / renewal conversation with the customer's procurement + economic-buyer pair, plus a one-page Good-Better-Best comparison handout. The deck enforces three irreducible disciplines drawn from Nagle's value-based pricing canon[^1], Ramanujam's *Monetizing Innovation*[^2], and the published B2B Elements of Value research[^3]:

1. **Realised Value before list price** — slide 03 quantifies what the customer has *actually* received in the past 12 months, in their own currency: hours saved, revenue enabled, risk avoided, headcount deferred. The list-price slide (06) does not appear until after this anchor is set.
2. **Willingness-to-Pay anchor** — slide 05 names the implied dollar-per-outcome ceiling (e.g. "$1 of platform spend = $4 of revenue enabled"). This is the conversation's gravity well; every subsequent offer is scored against it.
3. **Good-Better-Best with real fences** — slide 07 offers three packages with structural differences (volume tier, term length, premium SLA, additional modules), not three discount levels. The discount-only renewal is explicitly absent from the menu.
